## 2. AI Search Optimization (Generative Engine Optimization)

### 2.1 Understanding AI Search

AI search engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Copilot) work differently from traditional search:
- They **parse and understand content semantics**
- They **extract factual, structured information**
- They **prefer authoritative, well-cited sources**
- They **value clarity and directness**
- They **look for consistent NAP data across the web**

### 2.2 Content Optimization for AI

**Structure Content for AI Extraction:**

```markdown
# Question-Based Heading (H1)
Direct answer in first paragraph (2-3 sentences).

## Supporting Details (H2)
- Bullet points with key facts
- Statistics and data
- Specific service descriptions

## Examples and Case Studies (H2)
Concrete examples that AI can cite.

## FAQ Section (H2)
Question and answer format that AI can extract.
```

**Factual Density Optimization:**
- Include specific numbers: "20+ years", "1000+ clients", "98% satisfaction"
- Named entities: "Hedge Resource Centre", "Ashiaman", "Greater Accra"
- Dates and timelines: "since 2004", "founded in 2004"
- Service specifics: "6 core services", "50+ projects completed"

**Content Formats AI Prefers:**
1. **Definitions:** "HRC Ghana is a resource consulting firm that..."
2. **Lists:** "The services offered include: 1) Training, 2) Skills Development..."
3. **Comparisons:** "Unlike other firms, HRC Ghana..."
4. **Step-by-step:** "To book a consultation: Step 1... Step 2..."
5. **Data-driven:** "HRC Ghana has served 1000+ clients across 6 service areas..."

**Speakable Schema (Already Implemented):**

**Target Query Patterns:**

| Query Type | Example | Content Strategy |
|-----------|---------|------------------|
| Definition | "What is HRC Ghana?" | Clear definition in first paragraph |
| Service | "What services does HRC offer?" | Structured list with descriptions |

**Make Content Easy to Cite:**
- Include quotable statistics and facts

**Track AI Mentions:**
